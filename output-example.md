It grew from 40 to 320 firms in 9 months by embedding directly into Microsoft Word and Outlook, where lawyers already draft and negotiate. Its current workflow captures clause edits, fallback language choices, and negotiated outcomes, but most of that feedback is stored as unstructured comments and is not reused systematically.

**Question:**
Which strategy best maximizes LexLayers defensibility over the next 24 months, given the competitive and adoption dynamics?

(A) Match Microsoft feature-for-feature, cut price immediately, and rely on faster release cadence to retain market share while model quality converges.

(B) Reposition as the cheapest legal AI, migrate to aggressive freemium, and prioritize top-of-funnel growth to build distribution before incumbents saturate the market.

(C) Double down on Word/Outlook distribution partnerships only, defer data infrastructure work, and treat model/provider switching as the primary moat.

(D) Narrow to two high-value vertical workflows, convert negotiation feedback into structured proprietary outcome data, ship auditable trust artifacts, and price on measurable cycle-time/risk reduction instead of seat parity.

(E) Build an in-house foundation model immediately to avoid platform dependency, even if this delays product improvements and vertical packaging for 12-18 months.
